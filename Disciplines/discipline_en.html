Table Tennis

Sport's legacy

Table tennis, also known as ping-pong, is a racket sport that pits two or four players against each other around a table. Table tennis is a leisure activity, but it is also an Olympic sport since 1988. A sport of British origin marked by a series of technical evolutions (nature of racket coverings, introduction and then prohibition of fast glue) that have led to innovations in playing style (use of the “penhold grip” by Hungarians and then Asians) and in the tactics employed at the highest level such as the appearance of the topspin in the 1970s. Modern table tennis allows for a wide variety of playing systems, with attacking games, counter-initiative at the table or mid-distance, and particularly spectacular defensive games.

Mixed doubles

4 players participate in the event in teams of 2, of different sexes, around a table, which is no bigger than during the singles event. It is an extremely fast game, very tactical, and requires crazy coordination with your partner, as the ball must be hit alternately between the two teammates. There are 16 qualified teams in the draw, which starts in the round of 16.

How to play tennis table ?

Before starting the game, the two players begin with an adaptation period of two minutes maximum A game of table tennis generally takes place in three or four winning rounds (sometimes improperly called "set"). Each round is composed of a series of exchanges. A rally begins with a serve, also called "service" and ends when a player scores the point.
A round is won by the first player to score 11 points, with at least a 2-point lead over his opponent (Example: 11-9, 11-3 etc.). In the case where a player has only a 1-point lead, the round continues until there is a 2-point gap. (Example: 12-10, 15-13 etc.)

In tournaments, competitions or even the Olympics, the winner is the first to win 2 sets, and in the final, the first to win 3. At the end of each set, the players change sides, and the first serve of the set is given to the one who did not make the first serve of the previous set. As a result, throughout a game, the first serve of a set is always on the same side of the table. The first player to serve is determined by a draw.

HOW TO WIN ?

A point is earned by a player if his opponent:

  • fail to send back the ball ;
  • let the ball bounce more than once on their semi-table ;
  • prevents the ball from bounce on their semi-table (obstruction) ;
  • sends the ball back but it touches a obstacle before ;
  • sends the ball backwith something else than the racquet.

HOLD : Western players play mainly with a European racket grip. The handle is held in the hand, the index finger is stretched along the wood. Asian players play mainly in a penholder grip. The racket is held like a "penholder".

PLACING : Good players know how to use positioning to put themselves in a position of strength over their opponent, that is, to always be at the right distance from the ball when it arrives on their side of the table, and to force their opponent into a difficult position for them: too far from the table or too far to the side. This involves making them move so that they get tired, so that they are surprised or lose their accuracy; in general this consists of "walking" them (a consecrated term) from left to right of the table or forcing them to move backwards and then move forward quickly in the hope that they get tired or miss their return.

PLAY SYSTEM :

The attack game consists of taking as many risks as possible in order to outpace your opponent. The shots mainly used by attackers are topspin and hitting. The racket coverings used by attackers are very fast backsides with a thick layer of foam.

The defense game consists of provoking the opponent to make a mistake. It is indeed a question of returning the opponent's offensive shots (topspin and hitting) as often as possible so that the latter ends up making a mistake. This game encourages the use of backspin. The latter is effective when the defender masters the variation of effects: in fact, the defense can be cut, soft or topspin.

DIFFERENT HITS :

Strike: Theoretically, the player does not put any spin on the ball, the gesture is made flat and allows for a quick shot, to surprise the opponent and try to finish the point.

Backspin: A cut ball is hit by placing the racket almost horizontally and passing under the ball45. The racket starts from the top and moves downwards and forwards. The goal is to prevent the opponent from attacking in good conditions. Indeed, a cut ball tends to "dive" into the net after its rebound

Topspin: Topspin consists of "rubbing" the ball from bottom to top in order to give the ball a rotation from back to front. After rising, the ball will dive very quickly and accelerate upon touching the table, which is destabilizing for the opponent. A topspin exchange brings the ball to a speed approaching 200 km/h

Sidespin: As an alternative to topspin, side spin gives the ball a lateral effect in addition to the topspin. It is performed almost like a classic shot but the racket must rub the ball on the side, from back to front. This technique is difficult to perform but if the opponent is not attentive, he can easily be surprised because, instead of going up, the ball goes to the side.
